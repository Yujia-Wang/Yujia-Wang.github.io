OVERVIEW

PROBLEM

Mentoring can be a great way to share knowledge and help someone be successful in their personal or professional life. However, for mentors and mentees at Georgia Tech, there are several issues that are holding them back to build a great mentorship.

For mentors,

  • They don’t think they are good at anything so that they can’t be confident enough to mentor other people.
  • They feel upset and uncomfortable when mentees lack of independency.
  • They are often too busy to attend regular scheduled meetings.

For mentees,

  • They are having a hard time connecting with people who have necessary skills and are willing to mentor them.
  • They are worried that mentors will get upset if they ask too many questions.
  • They lack of motivations to keep practicing.

SOLUTION

GT Mentorship is an application designed to help Georgia Tech students get matched with right people for reciprocal mentorship. Every people is both mentor and mentee, providing and receiving help based on their interests. The main functions are:

  • Accurate matching system - everyone benefits from the reciprocal mentorship.
  • Efficient and effective working dashboard - facilitate collaboration and communication.
  • Skill evaluation system - help users gain confidence.
  • Achievement collection system - provide motivations to help users keep practicing.

For this design challenge, I will demonstrate my design process by answering “5W1H” questions.

RESEARCH

WHY AM I BUILDING THIS?

Mentoring can be a great way to share knowledge and help someone be successful in their personal or professional life. However, many potential mentors are often too busy to commit to regular meetings, or they have a hard time connecting with people seeking help. A better experience that helps prospective mentors and mentees get matched can facilitate individual growing and eventually create an interdependent growing community.

WHO AM I BUILDING IT FOR?

Mentorship includes two general categories of people: people who want to be a mentor, and people who want to be a mentee. However, depending on the context, the requirement for mentorship can vary a lot. Thus, I decided to narrow down my target user group to students at Georgia Tech for the following reasons:

  • College students is one step ahead to the society, so they need more help with their professional skills.
  • Mentorship is more effective when meeting in person, so narrowing down the scope to GT community can facilitate the communication.
  • Georgia Tech has a high concentration of various talented students studying in different fields, which means it has plenty of resources that can help people enhance their skills.

WHEN AND WHERE WILL IT BE USED?

After defining the target user group, I need to better understand their context and problems when they have a mentorship. I conducted interviews with 5 Georgia Tech students. Here are some interesting findings:

  • They would schedule a meeting first and then meet in person because it’s more efficient and effective.
  • They usually need a mentorship when they are new to something and hoping to keep improving it.
  • Since mentorship is more like an extracurricular activity and they are busy with college study, they don’t have a perfect plan and lack of motivations to attend regular meetings.
  • They would feel a sense of achievement when mentoring other people and can also get some self-improvement.

Then, I created an affinity mapping to organize my interview data.

After the affinity mapping, I identified several user needs of both mentors and mentees.

As a mentor,

  • I want to know what I am good at so that I can be more confident to teach other people.
  • I want to keep things efficient and effective so that I can feel more comfortable and easy to mentor other people.
  • I want to schedule meeting time more easily so that I can manage my time better.

As a mentee,

  • I want to find a suitable mentor more easily so that I can spend less time on searching and more time on developing the relationship.
  • I want to access the study resources more easily so that I don’t need to ask my mentor for favor all the time.
  • I want to be pushed to finish my work so that I can still keep practicing when I lack of motivations.

WHAT AM I BUILDING?

Ideally, the solution should include two platforms: mobile application and web application for the following reasons:

For the mobile application,

  • They need to communicate with each other efficiently and instantly.
  • They need to check their progress conveniently.

For the web application,

  • They need to transfer documents more easily.

Due to time limitation, this design challenge will only focus on the mobile application.

DESIGN

IDEATION & SKETCHING

To conduct a more efficient ideation, first, based on user needs, I established several design directions (assumptions) on how they may achieve that.

For mentors,

  • To know what they are good at, they need some evidence to prove their skills.
  • To keep things efficient and effective, they need a place to organize their resources.
  • To schedule meeting time more easily, they need a way to schedule collaboratively.

For mentees,

  • To find a suitable mentor more easily, they need to know the detailed information about the mentors, such as background, interests, and proficient skills.
  • To access the study resources more easily, they need a place to check the resources provided by mentors.
  • To be pushed to finish their work, they need to know what reward they will get when they finish on time.

Based on these directions (assumptions), I came with targeted solutions as follows:

For mentors,

  • A testing survey to help them evaluate their skills.
  • A function that they can upload and organize their resources.
  • A function that they can schedule their meetings with their mentees collaboratively.

For mentees,

  • A function that they can filter mentors as they need.
  • A function that they can check and organize the resources shared by their mentors.
  • An achievement collection system that can encourage them to finish their practice.

Since these ideas were still rough, I felt the need to specify them. Thus, I sketched them on paper to help me guide the design of the wireframe.
